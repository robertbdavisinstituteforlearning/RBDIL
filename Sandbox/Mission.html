RMDIL Mission and Objectives

Scholarly work at the Robert B. Davis Institute for Learning (RBDIL) focuses on the nexus of research and educational practice, particularly issues of learning and teaching in the content areas of mathematics and STEM learning. Its mission is twofold:
  1. to conduct detailed studies over time and across a variety of educational settings to identify how individual learners and groups of learners construct mathematical and scientific ideas and forms of reasoning; and

  2. to use findings and products of research in teacher education for promoting instructional practices through which students develop critical thinking and reasoning abilities grounded in their understanding of mathematics and science.

With a team of doctoral students, faculty and consultants, the objectives of RBDIL are:

  1. Oversee the RBDIL video cloud data collection of 5000+ hours.
    1. Assist doctoral students, researchers and faculty to retrieve video data for analysis. This involves working with current researchers and doctoral students who study video data for projects and dissertation work.
    2. Prepare identified videos for ingesting into the Video Mosaic repository (VMC). This requires completing metadata forms for searches as per requirements of Rutgers Library and making the video data available for ingesting into the VMC.
    3. Training for preparation of metadata requirements for video ingestion into the VMC, working collaboratively with Rutgers Library colleagues and staff.
    4. Pursue research grant with Rutgers University Libraries to build AI tools for cataloging data housed in IML Collection
    5. Publish video narratives on the VMC, scholarly journals and for use of teacher education. For example, US and China are the top two countries of origin where the website has visits.   2. Work with faculty, current students, and graduates in accessing video data for the creation of video narratives (VMCAnalytics); This will require ingesting new videos in to the VMC and completing metadata library requirements as per Rutgers Library requirements.
  3. Assist graduate students enrolled in GSE courses that require the creation of analytics to identify clips and or videos, as per their needs. This may require ingesting new videos into the VMC and one-on-one help.
  4. Assist new VMC users in learning how to use the RUanalytic tool.
  5. Assist scholars in identifying videos for creating VMCAnalytics for the special Journal of Mathematical Behavior issue, The Longitudinal Study this involves a set of 6-12 papers that will be linked to newly created VMCAnalytics.
  6. Monitor the receipt of Community Code of Contact forms for authors whose VMCAnalytics are accepted for publication; these forms are required and will involve monitoring about 40 submissions per year.
  7. Work with RBDIL Director to create and implement a review system for VMCAnalytics submitted for publication.
