Community Engagement

The RBDIL has a long history of community engagement based on partnerships and projects implemented with public and charter schools over many decades (1980s - 2020s). This work has included both long-term research partnerships and shorter-term professional development programs, although sometimes the latter type of work entailed a multi-year initiative with the school partner, and what began as professional development subsequently became research. That was the case for the Kenilworth Longitudinal Study, as well as the cross-sectional research that engaged school partners in New Brunswick and Colts Neck, all of which began with RBDIL providing supports for elementary teachers in giving their students mathematics lessons.

Levels of community engagement waxed and waned with the availability of funding sources for such work. Nationally and in New Jersey, there was far more funding for educator professional development in the 1990s and early 2000s than was made available from about 2006 onward. Sponsored projects through which the RBDIL engaged the K-12 educator community included the operation of a Regional Center for the New Jersey Statewide Systemic Initia3ve from 1992-2001 in which workshops and other training were delivered to improve the quality of teaching in math and science and incorporate technology into classroom instruction.

A longer and more focused partnership engaged school communities in the three urban districts of New Brunswick, Perth Amboy, and Plainfield over a period of eight years (1998-2006) through three rounds of funding cycles for teacher professional development in the content areas of language arts/literacy, mathematics, and science. Outcomes of that work included participating teachers pursuing graduate studies and earning master's degrees at the GSE. The enduring partnership with Plainfield Public Schools led to obtaining a two large NSF research grants, Research on Informal Math Learning (IML) and the MetroMath Center (with CMSCE and RU-N).


Community Building with New Jersey Schools

RBDIL, since its inception, has been committed to engaging in partnerships with New Jersey schools. The work involves teacher professional development and research studies, following teacher and student learning over time.
